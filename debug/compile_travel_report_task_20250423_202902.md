# Paris Travel Report: May 23 - May 30, 2025

## 1. Trip Overview

This report outlines a comprehensive travel plan for your trip to Paris, France, from May 23 to May 30, 2025. It incorporates insights from various travel specialists to provide a well-rounded guide covering transportation, accommodation, attractions, dining, and essential packing advice. This trip is designed for two travelers with a moderate budget, focusing on historical sites and local cuisine, with a mix of different travel styles.

**Executive Summary:**

*   **Transportation:** Opt for a direct flight from New York to Paris to minimize travel time. Utilize Paris's efficient Metro system for navigating the city. Consider a Navigo Easy pass for cost-effective travel within central Paris.
*   **Accommodation:** Stay in the Marais or Latin Quarter for central locations with easy access to historical sites and dining options.
*   **Attractions:** Prioritize visits to the Louvre Museum, Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame Cathedral, and explore the Latin Quarter's historical charm. Consider a day trip to Versailles.
*   **Dining:** Indulge in classic French cuisine at local bistros and cafes. Explore diverse culinary experiences in the Marais district.
*   **Weather & Packing:** Pack layers, including a waterproof jacket, as Paris experiences mild and potentially rainy weather in late May. Comfortable walking shoes are essential.

## 2. Transportation

### 2.1. Getting to Paris from New York

*   **Flight Options:**
    *   **Airlines:** Air France, Delta, United, American Airlines, and Norwegian offer flights from New York City (JFK, EWR, LGA) to Paris (CDG, ORY).
    *   **Estimated Prices:** $600 to $1500+ per person for a round-trip flight, depending on booking time and class.
    *   **Flight Duration:** 7-8 hours (direct). 10-15+ hours (connecting).
    *   **Number of Stops:** Direct flights are recommended where possible.
    *   **General Schedules:** Flights available throughout the day.
    *   **Airport Transportation:**
        *   **NYC Airports:** Taxis, ride-sharing (Uber/Lyft), airport shuttles, subway, and commuter rail.
        *   **Paris Airports:**
            *   **CDG:** RER B train (fast and economical), Roissybus, taxis, ride-sharing.
            *   **ORY:** Orlybus, Orlyval (connects to RER B), taxis, ride-sharing.

### 2.2. Local Public Transit in Paris

*   **Available Types:**
    *   **Metro:** Extensive and efficient network.
    *   **Bus:** Comprehensive, but can be slower.
    *   **Tram:** Serves the outskirts of Paris.
    *   **RER:** Connects Paris to suburbs, including airports and Versailles.
*   **Key Routes for Tourists:**
    *   **Metro Line 1:** Louvre Museum, Champs-Élysées, Arc de Triomphe.
    *   **Metro Line 4:** Notre Dame Cathedral, Latin Quarter.
    *   **RER C:** Eiffel Tower, Versailles.
*   **Transit Pass Options:**
    *   **Navigo Easy:** Contactless card, load with individual tickets or day passes.
    *   **Navigo Découverte:** Weekly or monthly pass (requires a photo). Best for longer stays.
    *   **Paris Visite Pass:** Unlimited travel for a specified number of days (1, 2, 3, or 5 days).
*   **Approximate Costs:**
    *   **Single "t+" ticket:** €2.10.
    *   **Navigo Easy (10 tickets):** €16.90.
    *   **Paris Visite Pass (1 day, zones 1-3):** €13.50.
*   **Operating Hours and Frequency:**
    *   **Metro:** 5:30 AM to 12:30 AM (later on weekends). Every 2-10 minutes during peak hours.
    *   **Bus:** 6:00 AM to 12:30 AM. Night buses ("Noctilien") operate from 12:30 AM to 5:30 AM.
