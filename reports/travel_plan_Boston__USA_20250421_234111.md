## 4. Local Guide

**1. Brief History & Fun Facts:**

Boston was founded in 1630 by English Puritan settlers and named after Boston, Lincolnshire in England. It quickly became the political, commercial, financial, religious, and educational center of Puritan New England. Boston played a pivotal role in the American Revolution, witnessing events like the Boston Massacre and the Boston Tea Party. The city boasts many firsts, including the nation's first public park (Boston Common), first public school (Boston Latin School), and first subway system. Today, Boston is a global leader in higher education, research, and biotechnology. It's also known for its strong economy, led by finance, IT, and government sectors, and for being a hub of innovation and entrepreneurship. Boston households provide the highest average rate of philanthropy in the nation.
